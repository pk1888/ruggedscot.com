# A Wee Guide to Scottish Words

If you’re not from Scotland, some of the words we use might leave you scratching your head.

Scots and Scottish slang have loads of great words that you won’t hear anywhere else. Some of them are funny, some of them are blunt, and some of them make absolutely no sense unless you grew up here.

For example, the word "cunt" is a really offensive word in some countries, but in Scotland it can be used quite casually depending on the context. You might hear someone say something like:

*Aye, that cunt over there.*

In that case it simply means something like:

*Yeah, that guy over there.*

Another example is the word "fanny". You might hear someone say something like:

*Uch, he's just a fanny.*

In that case it simply means something like:

*He's a right pain in the arse.*  
*He's an idiot.*

Or it can also be something pals say. If your pal is being a bit of a dick, you might say:

*Uch, he's just being a wee fanny.*

So really you can use it to show how much you dislike someone, or you can use it in a playful way about a pal.

Growing up I used to refer to my pals as **fanny baws** or **bawbag**. As I got older those types of words disappeared. I'm sure they are still used, but I haven't heard them in such a long time.

Another one is **tits**, you know, like *lassie's tits*. We can use this in many ways. For example, if you come across someone who is high on drugs, you can say:

*He's aff his tits.*

This would mean he is high on drugs.

Or you can say:

*He's a pure tit.*

Which would mean he is being a complete idiot.

Another good one is just **dick**, you know, the guy's junk. So for example if you're talking about a guy and want to say he's a bit of a dick, you can say:

*He's a right dick.*  
*He's a wee dick.*  
*He's a bit of a dickhead.*

Or in a more friendly way, a bunch of people I used to work with would greet each other like this:

*Awrite, ya wee dick?*

Which was just our way of saying hello. It caused zero offence — it was just a turn of phrase we used as a greeting.

Here’s a wee guide to some Scottish words and what they mean. Some places in Scotland use different words than others, so this is just a general guide. For example below you'll see the word **ken** — oh my fucking gawd, that word really gets on my tits!

## Common Scottish Words

**Wee**  
Meaning: Small  
Example: *I'll just have a wee look.*

**Braw**  
Meaning: Brilliant / great  
Example: *That's a braw view from up here.*

**Blether**  
Meaning: A long chat  
Example: *We stood having a blether for ages.*

**Messages**  
Meaning: Groceries or shopping  
Example: *I'm away for the messages.*

**Scran**  
Meaning: Food  
Example: *That was some good scran.*

**Gallus**  
Meaning: Bold, cheeky, confident  
Example: *He's a gallus wee lad.*

**Dreich**  
Meaning: Miserable, dull weather  
Example: *It's a dreich day the day.*

**Numpty**  
Meaning: Fool / idiot  
Example: *You absolute numpty.*

**Bampot**  
Meaning: Crazy person  
Example: *That guy's a total bampot.*

**Outwith**  
Meaning: Outside of  
Example: *That's outwith my control.*

## Everyday Scottish Words

**Aye**  
Meaning: Yes

**Naw**  
Meaning: No

**Ken**  
Meaning: Know  
Example: *D'ye ken what I mean?*

**Bairn**  
Meaning: Child

**Wean**  
Meaning: Child (wee one)

**Lass**  
Meaning: Girl

**Lad**  
Meaning: Boy

**Pure**  
Meaning: Very / extremely  
Example: *That's pure brilliant.*

**Dead**  
Meaning: Very  
Example: *That's dead funny.*

They might just be having a wee laugh.
